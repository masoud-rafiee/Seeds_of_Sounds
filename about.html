Where Stories Become Seeds of Change

We believe that every voice has the power to inspire. By capturing memories and pledges through retro hardware we foster community and imagine new possibilities.

Our Story

Seeds of Sound began in 2019 as a student‑led exploration into how emerging technologies can preserve the past while planting hope for the future. What started as a curiosity about rotary phones grew into a collaborative research project that bridges disciplines—from computer science and digital art to sustainability and sociology.

By retrofitting vintage telephones with microcontrollers, sensors and AI, we created an approachable interface for people of all ages to share stories and make pledges. Each recorded message is archived in a digital time capsule and represented as a seed in a virtual forest. As seeds sprout and bloom, participants receive reminders to act on their commitments. This simple metaphor transforms collective storytelling into tangible momentum for social and environmental change.

Our installations have appeared at university campuses, community events and international forums. Along the way we’ve learned that innovation doesn’t require abandoning the past—it flourishes when we reimagine and repurpose it.
